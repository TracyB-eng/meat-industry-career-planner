Careers within the NZ meat industry — click any role to expand

🔪
Process worker / boner / slicer
Floor operations · immediate entry
Entry level ›

Slaughter, dress, bone, trim and portion carcasses on a processing chain. Fast-paced, physically demanding team environment.

No formal qualifications required — full training provided on the job. A Gateway placement at school is a great head start.

$28–$42/hr (skilled operators). Union-negotiated rates apply.

Floor supervisor → shift manager → production manager. Also a strong step into quality assurance roles.

🥩
Butcher / retail butcher
Retail & specialty · apprenticeship
Apprenticeship ›

Cut, prepare and present meat for retail customers. Involves customer service, merchandising, and in-depth product knowledge.

3-year Butchery Apprenticeship — earn while you learn. Can start straight from school; NCEA Level 2 preferred.

$50,000–$85,000. Master butchers running their own shops can earn significantly more.

Head butcher → butchery manager → own specialty shop, deli, or charcuterie business.

🔬
Food technologist
R&D · product development · food science
University degree ›

Develop new meat products, improve processing methods, manage food safety, packaging and shelf life. Lab and computer work combined.

Bachelor of Food Technology (4 years + compulsory Honours). Massey, AUT, Lincoln and Waikato all offer this.

$49,000–$148,000. On NZ's long-term skill shortage list — excellent demand.

Senior food technologist → R&D manager → technical director. Strong international career potential.

📋
Quality assurance / food safety officer
Compliance · hygiene · export standards
Diploma or degree ›

Monitor and enforce hygiene, food safety, and compliance standards throughout the plant to meet NZ and international export requirements.

NZ Certificate in Food Processing (Level 4) via polytech, or a food science / technology degree. Some move up from the processing floor.

$55,000–$95,000 depending on plant size and level of seniority.

QA supervisor → QA manager → regulatory affairs specialist or compliance director.

🐄
Livestock / procurement officer
Farming liaison · supply chain · rural
Diploma or degree ›

Work with farmers, assess and source livestock, draft animals at yards, and liaise between farmers and processing companies.

NZ Diploma in Agribusiness Management, or a degree in Agriculture or Rural Valuation. Farm experience is highly valued.

$60,000–$100,000+. Includes company vehicle, phone and travel allowances.

Senior livestock rep → regional procurement manager → supply chain director.

🩺
Veterinarian (meat hygiene / official vet)
Animal health · MPI · export certification
University degree ›

Inspect animals before and after slaughter to certify meat is safe for human consumption and meets NZ and international export standards.

BVSc from Massey University (5 years). Highly competitive entry — strong marks in Biology and Chemistry essential.

$90,000–$150,000+. Official vets for MPI are among the best-compensated roles.

Senior official vet → MPI regulatory roles → international food safety consultancy.

📦
Sales, export & marketing
International trade · business development
Degree or experience ›

Sell NZ meat to buyers in Japan, UK, Europe, China and the Middle East. Manage customer relationships, pricing and export logistics.

Bachelor of Commerce, Business, or International Trade. Language skills (Japanese, Mandarin) are a significant advantage.

$65,000–$130,000+. Often includes international travel and commission.

Account manager → export manager → GM of sales. Senior roles at ANZCO, Silver Fern Farms, Alliance Group.

⚙️
Engineer (mechanical / refrigeration)
Plant operations · cold chain · automation
Degree or trades ›

Maintain, repair and upgrade processing machinery, cold chain refrigeration systems, and plant automation technology.

NZ Certificate in Engineering (Level 4) or BE degree. A refrigeration trades apprenticeship is also a direct route in.

$65,000–$120,000+. Refrigeration engineers are in particularly strong demand.

Senior engineer → engineering manager → plant operations director.

Your pathway from school into the industry

Now — Year 12 & 13
Build foundations at school
Focus on NCEA subjects in science, maths and agriculture. Look for a Gateway work placement at a meat processing plant or butchery. Complete STAR courses in food production if available. Talk to your careers adviser and visit careers.govt.nz.
NCEA Level 2+ Gateway programme STAR courses
After Year 13 — Vocational route
Apprenticeship, polytech or straight to work
Start a Butchery Apprenticeship (3 years, earn while you learn) or enrol in a Level 3–4 NZ Certificate in Food Processing at EIT, Wintec, NMIT or SIT. Alternatively, apply directly to ANZCO, Silver Fern Farms, Alliance Group or AFFCO for entry-level roles — full training is provided.
Butchery apprenticeship NZ Certificate Level 3–4 Direct employment
After Year 13 — University route
Enrol in a relevant degree
For food technology, science or veterinary roles: apply to Massey University (Palmerston North/Auckland), Lincoln, AUT or Waikato. University Entrance required — strong marks in Biology, Chemistry and Maths are essential for competitive programmes.
Massey University Lincoln University AUT / Waikato
Years 2–4
Gain experience & specialise
Polytech graduates enter the workforce with a Level 4–5 qualification. University students complete industry internships. Apprentices finish their qualification and may become multi-skilled operators or floor supervisors. The industry rewards people who keep learning.
Industry internships Graduate programmes On-the-job training
Years 5–10+
Senior, specialist & leadership roles
Progress into quality management, food science leadership, livestock procurement, export sales or plant engineering. NZ red meat exports over $4B annually — skilled professionals at every level are in strong, ongoing demand across the country.
Management Global export roles Consultancy

12-month action plan — tick off as you go

1
Explore & decide
Months 1–2
  • Visit careers.govt.nz and explore meat industry roles in depth
  • Talk to your school careers adviser about Gateway placements in meat processing
  • Decide between the vocational (polytech/apprenticeship) or university pathway
  • Contact ANZCO Foods, Silver Fern Farms or Alliance Group to ask about plant visits or school programmes
2
Plan your subjects
Months 3–4
  • Confirm Year 13 subject choices: Biology, Chemistry, Maths — or Agriculture if available
  • Apply for the Gateway programme or a STAR food processing course at school
  • If university path: research Massey, Lincoln, AUT or Waikato open days and entry requirements
  • If vocational path: contact your local polytech (EIT, Wintec, NMIT or SIT) for Level 3–4 food processing programmes
3
Get real experience
Months 5–8
  • Complete your Gateway placement at a meat plant if enrolled
  • Apply for holiday or part-time work at a local processing plant or butchery
  • Attend a Food Technology or Agriculture open day at a university campus
  • Look into Apprenticeship Boost subsidies at govt.nz if considering a butchery apprenticeship
4
Apply for your next step
Months 9–12
  • Submit university applications via NZUAC (opens October for the following year)
  • Apply to polytech programmes — most have rolling enrolment, apply early for February start
  • Research graduate or cadet programmes at ANZCO, Silver Fern Farms or Alliance Group
  • Set up a LinkedIn profile and connect with people already working in the meat industry
  • Apply for Student Allowance or StudyLink funding ahead of your start date
